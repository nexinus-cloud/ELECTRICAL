About Us

Electricians work with electrical power. We install, test, and maintain wiring, lighting systems, and fixtures in homes and businesses. We work indoors and outdoors. we work in new construction and in existing buildings. We should have a basic understanding of mathematics, including algebra, and good problem-solving skills.

Multi Meter

A Mulit Meter is a device that is used to measure the electricity consumption of a building. A multimeter is a measuring instrument that can measure multiple electrical properties. A typical multimeter can measure voltage, resistance, and current, in which case can be used as a voltmeter, ohmmeter, and ammeter. Some feature the measurement of additional properties such as temperature and capacitance.

A multimeter is a handheld measuring instrument used to measure various electrical properties such as voltage, current, and resistance in electrical circuits. It typically consists of a digital display screen, input jacks for connecting test leads or probes, and a selection dial to choose the desired measurement function. Multimeters are commonly used by electricians, technicians, and hobbyists for troubleshooting and testing electrical equipment and systems.

To use a multimeter, the device is typically connected to the circuit being tested using probe leads. The user then selects the desired measurement function on the dial, adjusts any necessary settings, and takes the measurement. Multimeters are versatile tools and are commonly used by electricians, electronic technicians, hobbyists, and DIY enthusiasts for troubleshooting, testing, and general maintenance of electrical and electronic systems.

1st Year Electrical Apprentice

A first-year electrical apprentice typically assists journeyman electricians with tasks such as installing and maintaining electrical systems, wiring, and equipment. They may also help with troubleshooting electrical issues, reading blueprints and technical drawings, handling tools and materials, and learning safety procedures. Additionally, they may attend classroom training to learn about the theory and principles of electrical systems.

2nd Year Electrical Apprentice

In their second year, electrical apprentices advance their skills by taking on more complex tasks such as installing fixtures, switches, and outlets, as well as wiring circuits. They also develop proficiency in troubleshooting electrical problems, assisting journeyman electricians in diagnosing issues with electrical systems. Furthermore, they deepen their understanding of electrical codes and regulations to ensure compliance and safety standards are met. Apprentices continue their education through classroom instruction, furthering their knowledge of electrical theory, mathematics, and safety practices. Additionally, they may start to take on leadership roles by supervising first-year apprentices or coordinating tasks on job sites, honing their communication and organizational skills. This year is pivotal for apprentices as they continue to grow and prepare for eventual certification as journeymen electricians.
